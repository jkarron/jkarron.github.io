Selected Works
IMDB Movies Project
For my Interactive Design and Development class, I created a page using HTML and CSS to tell the story of what people consider a "good" movie using IMDB as my base. It included learning HTML elements I was unfamiliar with, including Highcharts and Google Fusion Tables to create maps.
Germany World Cup Project
For my Interactive Design and Development class, I created a page using HTML and CSS to tell the story of Germany's 2014 World Cup Run. It included learning HTML elements I was unfamiliar with, including Highcharts and Google Fusion Tables to create maps.
Mammography Affordable Care Act Project
For my final project in both my interactive design and public affairs reporting course, I delved into Obamacare's effects on mammography rates. This website was developed in tandem with the article, which found that diagnoses of stage 1 breast cancer increased due to the ACA's preventive service mandate. This project required finding and interviewing sources, developing a website from scratch that told this story, and graphics that related to the interviews and premise of the story.
Slate Hang Up and Listen After Ball
This was my first foray into speaking during a podcast. With the help of Josh Levin (then Slate's executive editor) and Stefan Fatsis (author and HUAL commenter), I produced a spoken piece about the National Women's Hockey League's ups and downs during their innaugural season. My piece is at the hour and 21st minute mark.
Game of Thrones Video Project
For my final project in my Introduction to Multimedia Skills class, I produced a video about the Game of Thrones Pop Up Bar in downtown DC. This piece required forethought about which shots I wanted for my audience. It also tested my abiilty to shoot in a dark environment.
MedTech Boston Podcast
This was one of my favorite pieces because it tested my audio production skills. I used REAPER DAW editing software (which I taught myself how to use), and was able to create an accessible podcast for a start-up that wanted one for a long time.
NovaCaps Analytics vs the Eye Test
Sports is moving towards a more analytics based approach to evaluating players. This is one of my favorite pieces because it's a simple explainer for analytics. It required me to get down to the nitty-gritty of what certain statistics are used for, and put my hockey systems knowledge to the test.
MedTech Boston Ranked Health Article
This piece combined my love of health care reporting with a test of my ability to translate a legal language into common language. It required asking the right questions of interviewees instead of peripheral questions.
ArmChair All-Americans Referee Assessment
During the 2017 Stanley Cup Playoffs, the refereeing came under high scrutiny. My editor at ArmChair asked for my thoughts on what could improve the league, and this piece does just that. It took a critical eye and research capabilities to prove my points. It also required the ability to explain problems concisely for the average fan who wouldn't have known about the problems referees had during the playoffs.
