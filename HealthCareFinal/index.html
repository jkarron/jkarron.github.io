Technology Increased Incidence Rates

When mammography became the industry standard for indentifying breast cancer in the 80's the number of identified invasive breast cancers increased.

But this wasn't because more women had cancer: it was because better technology meant doctors could catch more cancers, the Surveillance, Epidemiology, and End Results Program said.

Mortality Rate Per Maryland County Due To Breast Cancer

Before the Affordable Care Act was implemented, women without insurance couldn't afford mammograms.

After its implementation, cancers were caught at earlier stages, leading to better health outcomes.

Maryland Followed the National Trend

Nationally, there was an increase in stage 1 identified breast cancers according to August's volume of Cancer Epidemiology.

Maryland women have experienced that same increase, with a percentage point jump in stage 1 identified breast cancers.

This chart shows that increase between the number of stage 1 identified breast cancers from 2006-2009 and 2011-2014.

Maryland's Black Women Followed National Trend

Black women in Maryland also saw an increase in stage 1 breast cancer.

The incidence of breast cancer among black women is almost the same as white women.

However, the mortality rate is nearly double.

This trend towards earlier screenigns could produce a drop in the mortalit rate of black women.

Those Without Insurance Suffer

If a woman lacks insurance, she is twice as likely to have not gotten a mammogram in the past two years.

For women who need access to mammograhy screenings, having insurance eliminates the financial barrier.

Under the ACA, there is no out-of-pocket cost for a mammogram because it falls under the preventive service mandate.
